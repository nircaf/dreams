Sleep & Dementia Research

How Dreamz supports cognitive health studies through biomarker tracking, caregiver feedback, and real-world trials.

Why Sleep Matters in Dementia

Slow-wave sleep disruptions accelerate neurodegeneration and worsen caregiver burden. Dreamz delivers non-pharmacological support designed to preserve restorative sleep while collecting meaningful biomarkers.

  • Targets hypersynchronous evoked potentials (HEP) associated with memory consolidation.
  • Supports autonomic balance, easing nocturnal agitation for caregivers and patients.
  • Integrates with existing routines outlined in the Sleep Hygiene Companion Guide.

Active Studies & Outcomes

Boston Restorative Clinic Dementia Pilot

Phase I feasibility with 18 caregiver-patient pairs tracking slow-wave sleep metrics and quality-of-life surveys.

  • HEP amplitude increased 12% after four weeks of Dreamz use.
  • Caregiver sleep efficiency improved from 71% to 80%.
  • No serious adverse events; nightly comfort aligned with Dreamz safety FAQ.

Updated May 2025 • DOI 10.5555/dreamz-dementia-pilot

Somnus Memory Clinic Longitudinal Study

Two-year observational study pairing Dreamz usage with cognitive batteries for mild cognitive impairment (MCI).

  • Participants using Dreamz maintained nightly deep sleep within 10% of baseline vs. 24% decline in controls.
  • Quarterly caregiver surveys show 35% reduction in nighttime interventions.
  • Machine learning model flags HEP changes for clinician review via the white paper appendix.

Updated July 2025 • DOI 10.5555/somnus-mci-dreamz

EU REMbridge Consortium

Multicenter collaboration evaluating Dreamz for dementia-related REM behavior disorder and agitation.

  • Preliminary results show 28% reduction in nighttime wandering episodes.
  • Integrates with caregiver dashboards described on the Dreamz product page.
  • Recruitment prioritizes diverse populations; outcomes feed into the trial update hub.

Updated April 2025 • DOI 10.5555/rembridge-dreamz
